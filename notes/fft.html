Fast Fourier Transform (FFT)

These notes present the discrete Fourier transform (DFT) as a linear operator on an $N$-dimensional Hilbert space and derive the radix-2 fast Fourier transform (FFT) factorization using Dirac notation. The key idea is to decompose the Hilbert space into even and odd subspaces, express the DFT in terms of two smaller DFTs, and then identify the block-matrix structure that leads to the familiar FFT butterfly.

I. Discrete Fourier transform as an operator

Let $\mathcal{H}_N$ be an $N$-dimensional Hilbert space with orthonormal basis

\[ \{\,\lvert 0\rangle,\lvert 1\rangle,\dots,\lvert N-1\rangle\,\}. \]

A vector (signal) is written as

\[ \lvert x\rangle = \sum_{n=0}^{N-1} x_n \lvert n\rangle. \]

The discrete Fourier transform is the linear map

\[ \lvert X\rangle = \hat{F}_N \lvert x\rangle = \sum_{k=0}^{N-1} X_k \lvert k\rangle, \]

with matrix elements

\[ \langle k\vert \hat{F}_N \vert n\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{N}}\, \omega_N^{kn}, \qquad \omega_N := e^{- 2\pi i / N}. \]

Therefore

\[ X_k = \langle k\vert \hat{F}_N \vert x\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{N}} \sum_{n=0}^{N-1} x_n \omega_N^{kn}, \qquad k = 0,\dots,N-1. \tag{1} \]

II. Even–odd decomposition of the Hilbert space

Assume $N$ is even, $N = 2M$. Then we can identify

\[ \mathcal{H}_N \cong \mathcal{H}_2 \otimes \mathcal{H}_M, \]

by mapping

\[ \lvert 2m\rangle \longleftrightarrow \lvert 0\rangle \otimes \lvert m\rangle, \qquad \lvert 2m+1\rangle \longleftrightarrow \lvert 1\rangle \otimes \lvert m\rangle, \quad m = 0,\dots,M-1. \tag{2} \]

A general state becomes

\[ \begin{aligned} \lvert x\rangle &= \sum_{n=0}^{N-1} x_n \lvert n\rangle = \sum_{m=0}^{M-1} x_{2m}\lvert 2m\rangle + \sum_{m=0}^{M-1} x_{2m+1}\lvert 2m+1\rangle \\ &= \lvert 0\rangle \otimes \left( \sum_{m=0}^{M-1} x_{2m}\lvert m\rangle \right) + \lvert 1\rangle \otimes \left( \sum_{m=0}^{M-1} x_{2m+1}\lvert m\rangle \right). \end{aligned} \tag{3} \]

Define the even and odd components

\[ \lvert x^{(e)}\rangle := \sum_{m=0}^{M-1} x_{2m}\lvert m\rangle, \qquad \lvert x^{(o)}\rangle := \sum_{m=0}^{M-1} x_{2m+1}\lvert m\rangle. \tag{4} \]

Then we can write

\[ \lvert x\rangle = \lvert 0\rangle \otimes \lvert x^{(e)}\rangle + \lvert 1\rangle \otimes \lvert x^{(o)}\rangle. \tag{5} \]

III. Radix-2 FFT: splitting into two $M$-point DFTs

Starting from Eq. (1), we split the sum over $n$ into even and odd parts:

\[ \begin{aligned} X_k &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{N}} \sum_{n=0}^{N-1} x_n \omega_N^{kn} \\ &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{N}} \left( \sum_{m=0}^{M-1} x_{2m}\omega_N^{k(2m)} + \sum_{m=0}^{M-1} x_{2m+1}\omega_N^{k(2m+1)} \right) \\ &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{N}} \left( \sum_{m=0}^{M-1} x_{2m}(\omega_N^2)^{km} + \omega_N^{k} \sum_{m=0}^{M-1} x_{2m+1}(\omega_N^2)^{km} \right). \end{aligned} \tag{6} \]

Note that

\[ \omega_N^2 = e^{-2\pi i (2)/N} = e^{-2\pi i /(N/2)} = \omega_{N/2} = \omega_M. \tag{7} \]

Define

\[ \tilde{E}_k := \sum_{m=0}^{M-1} x_{2m}\omega_M^{km}, \qquad \tilde{O}_k := \sum_{m=0}^{M-1} x_{2m+1}\omega_M^{km}. \tag{8} \]

Then Eq. (6) becomes

\[ X_k = \frac{1}{\sqrt{N}} \left( \tilde{E}_k + \omega_N^k \tilde{O}_k \right). \tag{9} \]

Now define the $M$-point DFT acting on the even and odd subsequences:

\[ E_k := \frac{1}{\sqrt{M}}\,\tilde{E}_k, \qquad O_k := \frac{1}{\sqrt{M}}\,\tilde{O}_k. \tag{10} \]

Since $N = 2M$, we have $\sqrt{M/N} = 1/\sqrt{2}$ and therefore

\[ X_k = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( E_k + \omega_N^k O_k \right), \qquad k = 0,\dots,M-1. \tag{11} \]

A similar calculation for $X_{k+M}$ gives

\[ X_{k+M} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( E_k - \omega_N^k O_k \right), \qquad k = 0,\dots,M-1. \tag{12} \]

Equations (11) and (12) are the standard radix-2 FFT “butterfly” relations.

IV. Operator form in Dirac notation

On $\mathcal{H}_N \cong \mathcal{H}_2 \otimes \mathcal{H}_M$, define

\[ \hat{G} := \hat{I}_2 \otimes \hat{F}_M. \tag{13} \]

Acting on $\lvert x\rangle$ from Eq. (5), we obtain

\[ \begin{aligned} \hat{G}\lvert x\rangle &= (\hat{I}_2 \otimes \hat{F}_M) \left( \lvert 0\rangle \otimes \lvert x^{(e)}\rangle + \lvert 1\rangle \otimes \lvert x^{(o)}\rangle \right) \\ &= \lvert 0\rangle \otimes \hat{F}_M\lvert x^{(e)}\rangle + \lvert 1\rangle \otimes \hat{F}_M\lvert x^{(o)}\rangle \\ &= \lvert 0\rangle \otimes \lvert E\rangle + \lvert 1\rangle \otimes \lvert O\rangle, \end{aligned} \tag{14} \]

where

\[ \lvert E\rangle := \hat{F}_M\lvert x^{(e)}\rangle = \sum_{k=0}^{M-1} E_k \lvert k\rangle, \qquad \lvert O\rangle := \hat{F}_M\lvert x^{(o)}\rangle = \sum_{k=0}^{M-1} O_k \lvert k\rangle. \tag{15} \]

Twiddle factor operator

Define the diagonal “twiddle” operator on $\mathcal{H}_M$,

\[ \hat{D} := \sum_{k=0}^{M-1} \omega_N^k\,\lvert k\rangle\langle k\rvert. \tag{16} \]

Then define

\[ \hat{T} := \lvert 0\rangle\langle 0\rvert \otimes \hat{I}_M + \lvert 1\rangle\langle 1\rvert \otimes \hat{D}. \tag{17} \]

Acting on Eq. (14),

\[ \hat{T} \left( \lvert 0\rangle \otimes \lvert E\rangle + \lvert 1\rangle \otimes \lvert O\rangle \right) = \lvert 0\rangle \otimes \lvert E\rangle + \lvert 1\rangle \otimes \hat{D}\lvert O\rangle. \tag{18} \]

Let

\[ \lvert O'\rangle := \hat{D}\lvert O\rangle = \sum_{k=0}^{M-1} \omega_N^k O_k \lvert k\rangle. \tag{19} \]

Hadamard on the first qubit

The Hadamard operator on $\mathcal{H}_2$ is

\[ \hat{H} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \big( \lvert 0\rangle\langle 0\rvert + \lvert 0\rangle\langle 1\rvert + \lvert 1\rangle\langle 0\rvert - \lvert 1\rangle\langle 1\rvert \big). \tag{20} \]

On $\mathcal{H}_2 \otimes \mathcal{H}_M$ we define

\[ \hat{U} := \hat{H} \otimes \hat{I}_M. \tag{21} \]

Acting on

\[ \lvert \psi\rangle := \lvert 0\rangle \otimes \lvert E\rangle + \lvert 1\rangle \otimes \lvert O'\rangle, \tag{22} \]

\[ \begin{aligned} \hat{U}\lvert \psi\rangle &= (\hat{H} \otimes \hat{I}_M) \left( \lvert 0\rangle \otimes \lvert E\rangle + \lvert 1\rangle \otimes \lvert O'\rangle \right) \\ &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left[ \lvert 0\rangle \otimes (\lvert E\rangle + \lvert O'\rangle) + \lvert 1\rangle \otimes (\lvert E\rangle - \lvert O'\rangle) \right]. \end{aligned} \tag{23} \]

Reading off the coefficients in the computational basis $\{\lvert 0\rangle\otimes\lvert k\rangle,\lvert 1\rangle\otimes\lvert k\rangle\}$, we recover precisely Eqs. (11)–(12) for $X_k$ and $X_{k+M}$.

Up to a simple permutation $\hat{P}$ that reorders the basis states between the “interleaved” and “even–odd” orderings, the resulting factorization is

\[ \hat{F}_N = \hat{U}\,\hat{T}\,(\hat{I}_2 \otimes \hat{F}_M)\,\hat{P}. \tag{24} \]

V. Explicit example: $N = 4$

For $N = 4$ we have $M = 2$ and

\[ \omega_4 = e^{-2\pi i / 4} = e^{-i\pi/2} = -i. \tag{25} \]

The $2 \times 2$ Fourier matrix is

\[ F_2 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{pmatrix}. \tag{26} \]

The $2 \times 2$ twiddle-factor matrix is

\[ D_2 = \mathrm{diag}(1,\omega_4) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -i \end{pmatrix}. \tag{27} \]

In the even–odd ordered basis $(x_0,x_2,x_1,x_3)^T$, the block-structured matrix is

\[ F_4^{(\mathrm{even\text{-}odd})} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \begin{pmatrix} F_2 & D_2 F_2 \\ F_2 & - D_2 F_2 \end{pmatrix}. \tag{28} \]

Evaluating $D_2 F_2$ and simplifying leads to the usual $4$-point DFT matrix (up to the permutation that reorders $(x_0,x_2,x_1,x_3)$ back to $(x_0,x_1,x_2,x_3)$). This makes the FFT structure manifest: $F_4$ is built from one $F_2$ acting on the even indices, one $F_2$ acting on the odd indices, a twiddle factor, and a final “butterfly” mixing step.
