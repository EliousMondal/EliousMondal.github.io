Variational polaron transform

These notes contain my derivation of the variational polaron transformation for a Frenkel exciton model with site-local harmonic baths. The LaTeX body from my original vPT_derivation.tex file is rendered here using MathJax, following the same conventions as in the other notes.

Lets take the general Frenkel exciton model \begin{align}\label{H_Frenkel_re} \hat{H} = \sum_{n} &\left( \varepsilon_n + \sum_\nu\frac{g_{n,\nu}^2}{2\omega_{n,\nu}^2}\right)|n\rangle\langle n| + \sum_{n}\sum_{m> n}\Delta_{nm} \left( |n\rangle\langle m| + |m\rangle\langle n| \right) \nonumber \\ & + \underbrace{\sum_{n, \nu} \frac{1}{2} \left( \hat{P}_{n, \nu}^2 + \omega_{n, \nu}^2 \hat{R}_{n, \nu}^2 \right)}_{\hat{H}_\mathrm{b}} \underbrace{-\sum_{n, \nu}g_{n, \nu} \hat{R}_{n, \nu} |n\rangle \langle n|}_{\hat{H}_\mathrm{sb}} \end{align} \subsection{Partial Polaron Transform} Lets define the Polaron transform operator as, \begin{equation} \hat{S} = \sum_{k,\mu} i f_{k,\mu} \hat{P}_{k,\mu} |k\rangle\langle k| \end{equation} The diagonal part remains untouched and we have \begin{equation} \boxed{e^{\hat{S}} |n\rangle\langle n| e^{-\hat{S}} = |n\rangle\langle n|} \end{equation} The off-diagonal part becomes, \begin{equation} e^{\hat{S}} |n\rangle\langle m| e^{-\hat{S}} = |n\rangle\langle m| + \left[ \hat{S}, |n\rangle\langle m|\right] + \frac{1}{2!} \left[ \hat{S}, |n\rangle\langle m|\right]_2 + ... \end{equation} The first commutator becomes, \begin{align} \left[ \hat{S}, |n\rangle\langle m| \right] &= \sum_{k,\mu} i f_{k,\mu} \left[ \hat{P}_{k,\mu} |k\rangle\langle k|, |n\rangle\langle m| \right] = \sum_{k,\mu} i f_{k,\mu} \hat{P}_{k,\mu} \left[ |k\rangle\langle k|, |n\rangle\langle m| \right] \nonumber \\ &= \sum_{k,\mu} i f_{k,\mu} \hat{P}_{k,\mu} \left( |k\rangle\langle k|n\rangle\langle m| - |n\rangle\langle m|k\rangle\langle k| \right) = \sum_{k,\mu} i f_{k,\mu} \hat{P}_{k,\mu} \Bigl( |k\rangle \delta_{kn} \langle m| - |n\rangle \delta_{mk} \langle k| \Bigl) \nonumber \\ &= \sum_{\mu} i f_{n,\mu} \hat{P}_{n,\mu} |n\rangle\langle m| - \sum_{\mu} i f_{m,\mu} \hat{P}_{m,\mu} |n\rangle\langle m| = \sum_{\mu} i \left( f_{n,\mu} \hat{P}_{n,\mu} - f_{m,\mu} \hat{P}_{m,\mu} \right) |n\rangle\langle m| \nonumber \\ &= i \left( \hat{X}_n - \hat{X}_m \right) |n\rangle\langle m| = i \hat{X}_{nm} |n\rangle\langle m| \end{align} where we defined $\hat{X}_n = \sum_{\mu} f_{n,\mu} \hat{P}_{n,\mu}$ and $\hat{X}_{nm} = \hat{X}_n - \hat{X}_m$. The second commutator can be computed as following, \begin{align} \left[ \hat{S}, |n\rangle\langle m| \right]_2 &= i\sum_{k,\mu} f_{k,\mu} \left[ \hat{P}_{k,\mu} |k\rangle\langle k| , i\hat{X}_{nm} |n\rangle\langle m| \right] = i^2\sum_{k,\mu} f_{k,\mu} \left( \left[ \hat{P}_{k,\mu} |k\rangle\langle k| , \hat{X}_{nm} \right] |n\rangle\langle m| + \hat{X}_{nm} \left[ \hat{P}_{k,\mu} |k\rangle\langle k| , |n\rangle\langle m| \right] \right) \nonumber \\ &= i^2\sum_{k,\mu} f_{k,\mu} \left( \left[ \hat{P}_{k,\mu} , \hat{X}_{nm} \right] |k\rangle\langle k|n\rangle\langle m| + \hat{X}_{nm} \hat{P}_{k,\mu} \left[ |k\rangle\langle k|, |n\rangle\langle m| \right] \right) = i^2\sum_{k,\mu} f_{k,\mu} \hat{X}_{nm} \hat{P}_{k,\mu} \Bigl[ |k\rangle\langle k|, |n\rangle\langle m| \Bigl] \nonumber \\ &= i^2\sum_{k,\mu} f_{k,\mu} \hat{X}_{nm} \hat{P}_{k,\mu} \Bigl( |k\rangle\langle k|n\rangle\langle m| - |n\rangle\langle m|k\rangle\langle k| \Bigl) = i^2\sum_{k,\mu} f_{k,\mu} \hat{X}_{nm} \hat{P}_{k,\mu} \Bigl( |k\rangle \delta_{kn} \langle m| - |n\rangle \delta_{mk} \langle k| \Bigl) \nonumber \\ &= i^2\sum_{\mu} f_{n,\mu} \hat{X}_{nm} \hat{P}_{n,\mu} |n\rangle \langle m| + \sum_{\mu} f_{m,\mu} \hat{X}_{nm} \hat{P}_{m,\mu} |n\rangle \langle m| = i^2\sum_{\mu} f_{n,\mu} \hat{X}_{nm} \left( \hat{P}_{n,\mu} - \hat{P}_{m,\mu} \right) |n\rangle \langle m| \nonumber \\ &= i^2\hat{X}_{nm} \hat{X}_{nm} |n\rangle \langle m| = i^2 \hat{X}_{nm}^2 |n\rangle \langle m| = i^2 \hat{X}_{nm}^2 |n\rangle \langle m| \end{align} Following the pattern we can write \begin{equation}\label{Hnm_PT} \boxed{e^{\hat{S}} |n\rangle\langle m| e^{-\hat{S}} = \sum_{a=0}^{\infty} \frac{(i\hat{X}_{nm})^a}{a!} |n\rangle\langle m| = e^{i\hat{X}_{nm}} |n\rangle\langle m|} \end{equation} For the bath operators the momentum operator commutes with any othe momentum operator, so it remains invariant under the polaron transform. Thus, \begin{equation} \boxed{ e^{\hat{S}} \hat{P}_{n,\nu} e^{-\hat{S}} = \hat{P}_{n,\nu} } \end{equation} For the bath position operator, we have \begin{equation} e^{\hat{S}} \hat{R}_{n,\nu} e^{-\hat{S}} = \hat{R}_{n,\nu} + \left[ \hat{S}, \hat{R}_{n,\nu} \right] + \frac{1}{2!} \left[ \hat{S}, \hat{R}_{n,\nu} \right]_2 + ... \end{equation} The first commutator can be computed as, \begin{align} \left[ \hat{S}, \hat{R}_{n,\nu} \right] &= \sum_{k, \mu} i f_{k,\mu} \left[ \hat{P}_{k,\mu} |n\rangle\langle n|, \hat{R}_{n,\nu} \right] = \sum_{k, \mu} i f_{k,\mu} \left[ \hat{P}_{k,\mu}, \hat{R}_{n,\nu} \right] |n\rangle\langle n| = \sum_{k, \mu} i f_{k,\mu} (-i)\delta_{kn}\delta_{\mu\nu} |n\rangle\langle n| \nonumber \\ &= f_{n,\nu} |n\rangle\langle n| \end{align} Since it no longer has any additional bath dependent term and $[\hat{S}, |n\rangle\langle n|] = 0$, all the higher order commutators vanish and we get, \begin{equation} \boxed{e^{\hat{S}} \hat{R}_{n,\nu} e^{-\hat{S}} = \hat{R}_{n,\nu} + f_{n,\nu} |n\rangle\langle n|} \end{equation} The bath hamiltonian can now be transformed as \begin{align} e^{\hat{S}} \hat{H}_{\mathrm{b}} e^{-\hat{S}} &= \frac{1}{2} \sum_{n, \nu} e^{\hat{S}} \left( \hat{P}_{n, \nu}^2 + \omega_{n, \nu}^2 \hat{R}_{n, \nu}^2 \right) e^{-\hat{S}} = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{n, \nu} \left\{ e^{\hat{S}} \left( \hat{P}_{n, \nu}^2 \right) e^{-\hat{S}} + \omega_{n, \nu}^2 e^{\hat{S}} \left( \hat{R}_{n, \nu}^2 \right) e^{-\hat{S}} \right\} \nonumber \\ &= \frac{1}{2} \sum_{n, \nu} \left\{ \hat{P}_{n, \nu}^2 + \omega_{n, \nu}^2 \left( e^{\hat{S}} \hat{R}_{n, \nu} e^{-\hat{S}} \right) \left( e^{\hat{S}} \hat{R}_{n, \nu} e^{-\hat{S}} \right) \right\} \nonumber \\ &= \frac{1}{2} \sum_{n, \nu} \left\{ \hat{P}_{n, \nu}^2 + \omega_{n, \nu}^2 \left( \hat{R}_{n,\nu} + f_{n,\nu} |n\rangle\langle n| \right) \left( \hat{R}_{n,\nu} + f_{n,\nu} |n\rangle\langle n| \right) \right\} \nonumber \\ &= \frac{1}{2} \sum_{n, \nu} \left\{ \hat{P}_{n, \nu}^2 + \omega_{n, \nu}^2 \left( \hat{R}_{n,\nu}^2 + 2f_{n,\nu}\hat{R}_{n,\nu} |n\rangle\langle n| + f_{n,\nu}^2 |n\rangle\langle n| \right) \right\} \nonumber \\ &= \frac{1}{2} \sum_{n, \nu} \left( \hat{P}_{n, \nu}^2 + \omega_{n, \nu}^2 \hat{R}_{n,\nu}^2 \right) + \sum_{n, \nu} f_{n,\nu} \omega_{n, \nu}^2 \hat{R}_{n,\nu} |n\rangle\langle n| + \frac{1}{2} \sum_{n, \nu} \omega_{n, \nu}^2 f_{n,\nu}^2 |n\rangle\langle n| \nonumber \\ &= \hat{H}_{\mathrm{b}} + \sum_{n, \nu} f_{n,\nu} \omega_{n, \nu}^2 \hat{R}_{n,\nu} |n\rangle\langle n| + \frac{1}{2} \sum_{n, \nu} \omega_{n, \nu}^2 f_{n,\nu}^2 |n\rangle\langle n| \end{align} The system bath Hamiltonian gets transformed as \begin{align} e^{\hat{S}} \hat{H}_{\mathrm{sb}} e^{-\hat{S}} &= -\sum_{n, \nu}g_{n, \nu} \left( e^{\hat{S}} \hat{R}_{n, \nu} e^{-\hat{S}} \right) |n\rangle \langle n| = -\sum_{n, \nu}g_{n, \nu} \left( \hat{R}_{n, \nu} + f_{n,\nu} |n\rangle\langle n| \right) |n\rangle \langle n| \nonumber \\ &= -\sum_{n, \nu} g_{n, \nu} \hat{R}_{n, \nu} |n\rangle\langle n| - \sum_{n, \nu} g_{n, \nu} f_{n,\nu} |n\rangle\langle n| \end{align} Combining both we get, \begin{align} e^{\hat{S}} \left( \hat{H}_{\mathrm{b}} + \hat{H}_{\mathrm{sb}} \right) e^{-\hat{S}} &= \hat{H}_{\mathrm{b}} + \sum_{n, \nu} f_{n,\nu} \omega_{n, \nu}^2 \hat{R}_{n,\nu} |n\rangle\langle n| + \frac{1}{2} \sum_{n, \nu} \omega_{n, \nu}^2 f_{n,\nu}^2 |n\rangle\langle n| -\sum_{n, \nu} g_{n, \nu} \hat{R}_{n, \nu} |n\rangle\langle n| - \sum_{n, \nu} g_{n, \nu} f_{n,\nu} |n\rangle\langle n| \nonumber \\ &= \hat{H}_{\mathrm{b}} + \sum_{n, \nu} \left( f_{n,\nu} \omega_{n, \nu}^2 - g_{n, \nu} \right) \hat{R}_{n,\nu} |n\rangle\langle n| + \sum_{n, \nu} \left( \frac{\omega_{n, \nu}^2 f_{n,\nu}^2}{2} - g_{n, \nu} f_{n,\nu} \right) |n\rangle\langle n| \end{align} Performing a completion of square for the last term we obtain, \begin{align} &\hat{H}_{\mathrm{b}} + \sum_{n, \nu} \Bigl( f_{n,\nu} \omega_{n, \nu}^2 - g_{n, \nu} \Bigl) \hat{R}_{n,\nu} |n\rangle\langle n| + \sum_{n, \nu} \frac{\omega_{n, \nu}^2}{2}\left( f_{n,\nu}^2 - 2 \frac{g_{n, \nu}}{\omega_{n, \nu}^2} f_{n,\nu} + \frac{g_{n, \nu}^2}{\omega_{n, \nu}^4} - \frac{g_{n, \nu}^2}{\omega_{n, \nu}^4} \right) |n\rangle\langle n| \nonumber \\ &= \hat{H}_{\mathrm{b}} + \sum_{n, \nu} \Bigl( f_{n,\nu} \omega_{n, \nu}^2 - g_{n, \nu} \Bigl) \hat{R}_{n,\nu} |n\rangle\langle n| + \sum_{n, \nu} \frac{\omega_{n, \nu}^2}{2}\left( f_{n,\nu} - \frac{g_{n, \nu}}{\omega_{n, \nu}^2} \right)^2 |n\rangle\langle n| - \sum_{n, \nu} \frac{g_{n, \nu}^2}{2 \omega_{n, \nu}^2} |n\rangle\langle n| \end{align} Adding the above to the diagonal part of the system Hamiltonian we see that the last term exactly cancels the reorganization energy of the initial system Hamiltonian. The polaron transform of the diagonal part of the Hamiltonian becomes, \begin{align} e^{\hat{S}} \hat{H}_{\mathrm{d}} e^{-\hat{S}} &= \sum_{n} \left( \varepsilon_n + \sum_\nu\frac{g_{n,\nu}^2}{2\omega_{n,\nu}^2}\right)|n\rangle\langle n| + \sum_{n, \nu} \left\{ \frac{\omega_{n, \nu}^2}{2}\left( f_{n,\nu} - \frac{g_{n, \nu}}{\omega_{n, \nu}^2} \right)^2 - \frac{g_{n, \nu}^2}{2 \omega_{n, \nu}^2} \right\} |n\rangle\langle n| \nonumber \\ &\hspace{2cm} + \hat{H}_{\mathrm{b}} + \sum_{n, \nu} \Bigl( f_{n,\nu} \omega_{n, \nu}^2 - g_{n, \nu} \Bigl) \hat{R}_{n,\nu} |n\rangle\langle n| \nonumber \\ &= \sum_{n} \left\{ \varepsilon_n + \sum_\nu \frac{\omega_{n, \nu}^2}{2}\left( f_{n,\nu} - \frac{g_{n, \nu}}{\omega_{n, \nu}^2} \right)^2 \right\}|n\rangle\langle n| + \sum_{n, \nu} \Bigl( f_{n,\nu} \omega_{n, \nu}^2 - g_{n, \nu} \Bigl) \hat{R}_{n,\nu} |n\rangle\langle n| + \hat{H}_{\mathrm{b}} \end{align} The off-diagonal part of the system Hamiltonian gets transformed as, \begin{align} e^{\hat{S}} \hat{H}_{\mathrm{off}} e^{-\hat{S}} &= \sum_{n}\sum_{m > n}\Delta_{n,m} e^{\hat{S}} \Bigl( |n\rangle\langle m| + |m\rangle\langle n| \Bigl) e^{-\hat{S}} = \sum_{n}\sum_{m > n}\Delta_{n,m} \Bigl( e^{i\hat{X}_{nm}}|n\rangle\langle m| + e^{-i\hat{X}_{nm}}|m\rangle\langle n| \Bigl) \end{align} Thus the polaron transform of the Frenkel-exciton Hamiltonian becomes, \begin{align}\label{pPT_HFrenkel} \hat{H}_{\mathrm{PT}} = e^{\hat{S}} \hat{H} e^{-\hat{S}} &= \sum_{n} \left\{ \varepsilon_n + \sum_\nu \frac{\omega_{n, \nu}^2}{2}\left( f_{n,\nu} - \frac{g_{n, \nu}}{\omega_{n, \nu}^2} \right)^2 \right\}|n\rangle\langle n| + \sum_{n, \nu} \Bigl( f_{n,\nu} \omega_{n, \nu}^2 - g_{n, \nu} \Bigl) \hat{R}_{n,\nu} |n\rangle\langle n| \nonumber \\ & \sum_{n}\sum_{m > n}\Delta_{n,m} \Bigl( e^{i\hat{X}_{nm}}|n\rangle\langle m| + e^{-i\hat{X}_{nm}}|m\rangle\langle n| \Bigl) + \sum_{n, \nu} \frac{1}{2} \left( \hat{P}_{n, \nu}^2 + \omega_{n, \nu}^2 \hat{R}_{n, \nu}^2 \right) \end{align} \subsection{Gibbs-Bogoliubov-Feynman free energy} We divide the above Hamiltonian into a non-interacting ($\hat{H}_{0}$) and interacting Hamiltonian ($\hat{H}_{I}$), $\hat{H}_{\mathrm{PT}} = \hat{H}_{0} + \hat{H}_{I}$, \begin{equation} \hat{H}_{0} = \sum_{n} \left\{ \varepsilon_n + \sum_\nu \frac{\omega_{n, \nu}^2}{2}\left( f_{n,\nu} - \frac{g_{n, \nu}}{\omega_{n, \nu}^2} \right)^2 \right\}|n\rangle\langle n| + \sum_{n}\sum_{m > n} \kappa_{n,m} \Delta_{n,m} \Bigl( |n\rangle\langle m| + |m\rangle\langle n| \Bigl) + \hat{H}_{\mathrm{b}} \end{equation} and \begin{align} \hat{H}_{I} &= \sum_{n, \nu} \Bigl( f_{n,\nu} \omega_{n, \nu}^2 - g_{n, \nu} \Bigl) \hat{R}_{n,\nu} |n\rangle\langle n| + \sum_{n}\sum_{m > n}\Delta_{n,m} \Bigl( e^{i\hat{X}_{nm}}|n\rangle\langle m| + e^{-i\hat{X}_{nm}}|m\rangle\langle n| \Bigl) \nonumber \\ &\hspace{3cm}- \sum_{n}\sum_{m > n} \kappa_{n,m} \Delta_{n,m} \Bigl( |n\rangle\langle m| + |m\rangle\langle n| \Bigl) \end{align} where we defined a thermal averaged renormalisation factor for the inter-site off-diagonal cross-coupling as, \begin{equation}\label{vPT_renormFactor} \kappa_{n,m} = \mathrm{Tr} \left[ e^{i\hat{X}_{nm}} e^{-\beta \hat{H}_{\mathrm{b}}} \right] = \mathrm{Tr} \left[ e^{-i\hat{X}_{nm}} e^{-\beta \hat{H}_{\mathrm{b}}} \right] = \langle e^{\pm i\hat{X}_{nm}} \rangle_{\hat{H}_{\mathrm{b}}} \end{equation} By construction we not have \begin{equation}\label{Avg_HI} \langle \hat{H}_{I} \rangle_{\hat{H}_{\mathrm{b}}} = \mathrm{Tr} \left[ \hat{H}_{I} e^{-\beta \hat{H}_{\mathrm{b}}} \right] = 0 \end{equation} The Gibbs-Bogoliubov-Feynman upper bound on the free energy is described by, \begin{equation} A_B = -\frac{1}{\beta} \ln \left( \mathrm{Tr} \left\{ e^{-\beta \hat{H}_0} \right\} \right) + \langle \hat{H}_{I} \rangle_{\hat{H}_{\mathrm{b}}} %+ \mathcal{O} \left( \langle \hat{H}_{I}^2 \rangle_{\hat{H}_{\mathrm{b}}} \right) \end{equation} Using Eq.~\ref{Avg_HI}, this becomes, \begin{equation}\label{GBF_FreeEnergy} A_B = -\frac{1}{\beta} \ln \left( \mathrm{Tr} \left\{ e^{-\beta \hat{H}_0} \right\} \right) \end{equation} Let us evaluate the thermal average of the renormalization factor for a simple Frenkel-biexciton model. Since the bath Hamiltonian is separable for different sites, we can write $\hat{H}_{\mathrm{b}} = \hat{H}_{1,\mathrm{b}} \otimes \hat{\mathds{1}}_2 + \hat{\mathds{1}}_1 \otimes \hat{H}_{2,\mathrm{b}}$ where $\hat{H}_{n,\mathrm{b}}$ is the bath Hamiltonian for $n^{\mathrm{th}}$ excitonic site. \begin{align} \kappa_{1,2} &= \mathrm{Tr} \left\{ \exp \left( i\left[ \hat{X}_{1} \otimes \hat{\mathds{1}}_2 - \hat{\mathds{1}}_1 \otimes \hat{X}_{2} \right] \right) \exp \left( -\beta \left[ \hat{H}_{1,\mathrm{b}} \otimes \hat{\mathds{1}}_2 + \hat{\mathds{1}}_1 \otimes \hat{H}_{2,\mathrm{b}} \right] \right) \right\} \nonumber \\ % &= \mathrm{Tr} \left\{ \exp \left( \left[ i \hat{X}_{1} -\beta \hat{H}_{1,\mathrm{b}} \right] \otimes \hat{\mathds{1}}_2 \right) \exp \left( \hat{\mathds{1}}_{1} \otimes \left[-i\hat{X}_{2} -\beta \hat{H}_{2,\mathrm{b}}\right] \right) \right\} \nonumber \\ \end{align} Now lets try to evaluate the exponential containing a tensor product between an operator in one subspace with an identity in another subspace, \begin{align} \exp \left( \hat{A}_1 \otimes \hat{\mathds{1}}_2 \right) &= \hat{\mathds{1}}_1 \otimes \hat{\mathds{1}}_2 + \hat{A}_1 \otimes \hat{\mathds{1}}_2 + \frac{1}{2!} \left( \hat{A}_1 \otimes \hat{\mathds{1}}_2 \right)^2 + ... = \hat{\mathds{1}}_1 \otimes \hat{\mathds{1}}_2 + \hat{A}_1 \otimes \hat{\mathds{1}}_2 + \frac{1}{2!} \hat{A}_1^2 \otimes \hat{\mathds{1}}_2 + ... \nonumber \\ % &= \left( \hat{\mathds{1}}_1 + \hat{A}_1 + \frac{1}{2!} \hat{A}_1^2 + ... \right) \otimes \hat{\mathds{1}}_2 = e^{\hat{A}} \otimes \hat{\mathds{1}}_2 \end{align} Thus we can now write, \begin{align} \kappa_{1,2} &= \mathrm{Tr} \left\{ \left( \exp \left( \left[ i \hat{X}_{1} -\beta \hat{H}_{1,\mathrm{b}} \right] \right) \otimes \hat{\mathds{1}}_2 \right) \cdot \left(\hat{\mathds{1}}_{1} \otimes \exp \left( \left[-i\hat{X}_{2} -\beta \hat{H}_{2,\mathrm{b}}\right] \right) \right) \right\} \end{align} Using the identity $\left(\hat{A}_1 \otimes \hat{\mathds{1}}_2 \right) \cdot \left( \hat{\mathds{1}}_1 \otimes \hat{B}_2 \right) = \hat{A} \otimes \hat{B}$, we get \begin{align} \kappa_{1,2}&= \mathrm{Tr} \left\{ \exp \left( \left[ i \hat{X}_{1} -\beta \hat{H}_{1,\mathrm{b}} \right] \right) \otimes \exp \left( \left[-i\hat{X}_{2} -\beta \hat{H}_{2,\mathrm{b}}\right] \right) \right\} \nonumber \\ % &= \mathrm{Tr} \left\{ \exp \left( i \hat{X}_{1} \right) \exp \left(-\beta \hat{H}_{1,\mathrm{b}} \right) \right\} \cdot \mathrm{Tr}\left\{ \exp \left(-i\hat{X}_{2} \right) \left(-\beta \hat{H}_{2,\mathrm{b}} \right) \right\} \end{align} where in the last line we used the property $\mathrm{Tr}(\hat{A} \otimes \hat{B}) = \mathrm{Tr}(\hat{A}) \cdot \mathrm{Tr}(\hat{B})$. Using Eq.~\ref{vPT_renormFactor}, we thus get \begin{equation} \boxed{\kappa_{1,2} = \kappa_1 \cdot \kappa_2} \end{equation} where we define $\kappa_n = \mathrm{Tr}(e^{\pm i\hat{X}_n}e^{-\beta \hat{H}_{n,b}})$ as the renormalization contribution from the $n^{\mathrm{th}}$ exciton. \subsection{Renormalization of inter-site coupling} Let us try to analyze the bath momentum dependent term in the polaron transformed inter-site coupling. \begin{align} \exp \left( i\hat{X}_1 \right) = \exp \left( i\sum_\nu f_{1,\nu} \hat{P}_{1, \nu} \right) = \prod_{\nu} \exp \left( i f_{1,\nu} \hat{P}_{1,\nu} \right) = \prod_{\nu} \hat{D}_{\nu} \Bigl( i f_{1,\nu} \Bigl) \end{align} where $\hat{D}_{\nu}$ is the displacement operator for the $\nu_{\mathrm{th}}$ mode, generating a position shift of $f_{1,\nu}$. To compute $\kappa_1$ with this, let us first try to evaluate the matrix elements of the density matrix of a thermal bath in the position representation. \begin{align} \langle x| \hat{\rho}_{\mathrm{th}} | x' \rangle &= \frac{1}{Z} \langle x| e^{-\beta \hat{H}_{\mathrm{B}}} | x' \rangle = \frac{1}{Z} \sum_{nm} \langle x|n \rangle \langle n| e^{-\beta \hat{H}_{\mathrm{B}}} | m\rangle\langle m|x' \rangle = \frac{1}{Z} \sum_{nm} \langle x|n \rangle e^{-\beta \omega \left( n + \frac{1}{2} \right)} \langle n|m \rangle \langle m|x' \rangle \nonumber \\ &= \frac{1}{Z} \sum_{nm} \langle x|n \rangle e^{-\beta \omega \left( n + \frac{1}{2} \right)} \delta_{nm} \langle m|x' \rangle = \frac{1}{Z} \sum_{n} \langle x|n \rangle e^{-\beta \omega \left( n + \frac{1}{2} \right)} \langle n|x' \rangle = \frac{1}{Z} \sum_{n} e^{-\beta \omega \left( n + \frac{1}{2} \right)} \psi_{n}(x) \psi_{n}^*(x') \end{align} Now we know that \begin{equation} \psi_n(x) = \left( \frac{\omega}{\pi} \right)^\frac{1}{4} \left( \frac{1}{2^n n!} \right)^{\frac{1}{2}} e^{-\omega x^2 / 2} H_{n}\left(\sqrt{\omega} x\right) \end{equation} We can thus write, \begin{align} \langle x| \hat{\rho}_{\mathrm{th}} | x' \rangle &= \frac{e^{-\beta\omega/2}}{Z} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(e^{-\beta \omega})^n}{2^n n!} \left(\frac{\omega}{\pi}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}} e^{-\frac{\omega}{2} \left( x^2 + x'^2 \right)} H_{n}\left( \sqrt{\omega}x \right) H_{n}\left( \sqrt{\omega}x' \right) \nonumber \\ % &= \frac{e^{-\beta\omega/2}}{Z} \left(\frac{\omega}{\pi}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}} e^{-\frac{\omega}{2} \left( x^2 + x'^2 \right)} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \left( \frac{e^{-\beta \omega}}{2} \right)^n \frac{1}{n!} H_{n}\left( \sqrt{\omega}x \right) H_{n}\left( \sqrt{\omega}x' \right) \end{align} Now, according to Mehler's identity, \begin{equation} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \left( \frac{\rho}{2} \right)^2 \frac{1}{n!} H_{n}(a) H_{n}(b) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-\rho^2}} \exp \left\{
