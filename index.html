About

I am broadly interested in theoretical and computational chemistry, with a focus on non-equilibrium dynamics in molecular and condensed-phase systems. My work involves developing and applying mixed quantum–classical methods, Green’s-function techniques, and numerical algorithms tailored for large-scale simulations.

I enjoy building open-source tools for quantum dynamics and spectroscopy, mentoring students, and collaborating across chemistry, physics, and applied mathematics.

Research

  • Exciton–polaritons and cavity-modified chemistry

    Modeling linear and nonlinear spectra of molecular ensembles strongly coupled to optical cavities, including polaron decoupling, motional narrowing, and multi-mode cavity effects.

  • Mixed quantum–classical dynamics

    Developing and benchmarking mapping-based approaches (PLDM, Ehrenfest, etc.) and hybrid schemes that combine trajectory methods with master-equation/Lindblad formalisms.

  • Open quantum systems & spectral densities

    Using correlation-function approaches, reaction-coordinate mappings, and pseudomode models to treat complex environments described by Drude–Lorentz and Brownian spectral densities.
